<div className="text-justify">

  [Australia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Australian_Intelligence_Community),

  partnerships like the "*Nine Eyes*" and "*Fourteen Eyes*" alliances extend
  this surveillance reach, encompassing European and [non-European
  states](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Security_and_Intelligence_Division#:~:text=The%20Security%20and%20Intelligence%20Division,and%20national%20interests%20of%20Singapore.).
  These alliances share intelligence, making the boundaries between national
  privacy laws increasingly irrelevant.

### Whistleblowers: Heroes or Villains?

<div className="text-justify">
  The dark underbelly of mass surveillance has been brought to light by
  whistleblowers like [Edward
  Snowden](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edward_Snowden) and [Julian
  Assange](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Julian_Assange). Snowden, a former NSA
  contractor, revealed the pervasive spying programs of the United States and
  its allies. His disclosures in 2013 exposed how tools like
  [*PRISM*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PRISM) and
  [*XKeyscore*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/XKeyscore) allowed the NSA to
  monitor global communications, violating the privacy of millions.
</div>

<div className="text-justify">
  Snowden's revelations led to global debates about surveillance reform but also
  showcased the severe risks faced by whistleblowers. Exiled in Russia, Snowden
  remains a fugitive, despite his revelations sparking meaningful legal changes
  like the [Freedom Act](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/USA_Freedom_Act).

  abuses](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5rXPrfnU3G0) during the [Iraq
  War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iraq_War) but resulted in severe
  imprisonment and public backlash.
</div>

### Legislative Shortcomings

<div className="text-justify">
Both the United States and Europe claim to value whistleblower protections, yet their legislative frameworks often fall short. In the U.S., laws like the Whistleblower Protection Act exclude those who expose classified information, leaving individuals like Snowden vulnerable to prosecution under the Espionage Act. In Europe, the EU Whistleblower Protection Directive aims to safeguard those who report wrongdoing but has faced inconsistent implementation across member states.

These legal gaps discourage whistleblowers, allowing mass surveillance programs to persist unchallenged. While whistleblower protection laws exist on paper, their failure to address the complexities of exposing state surveillance ensures that accountability remains elusive.
</div>

### A Call for Accountability

<div className="text-justify">
  Mass surveillance undermines the fundamental rights of privacy and freedom

  surveillance programs. At the same time, stronger protections for
  whistleblowers are essential to ensure that those who expose wrongdoing are
  not punished for their bravery.
</div>

<div className="text-justify">
